This is one of the most common — and most frustrating — experiences for women, and it's completely normal. In the luteal phase (week 3–4 of your cycle), progesterone rises and then drops sharply before your period. This hormonal shift causes your body to retain water, which can add anywhere from 1 to 5 pounds on the scale. This is water weight, not fat. Your kidneys are responding to hormonal signals to hold onto sodium and water. Additionally, progesterone can slow digestion slightly, adding to that bloated, heavy feeling. HerRhythm tracks this pattern over multiple cycles so you can clearly see your true weight trend — separate from the hormonal noise. Once you see the pattern, the pre-period spike stops feeling like failure and starts feeling like data.
Your cycle has four distinct phases, each driven by different hormones. Menstrual (Week 1): Estrogen and progesterone are at their lowest. Energy may feel low, but inflammation is also dropping — this is your body's true reset. Follicular (Week 2): Estrogen rises steadily. This is typically your highest-energy, clearest-thinking phase. Appetite is naturally lower, and your body responds well to exercise. Ovulation (Week 3): A brief estrogen peak triggers ovulation. Energy is high, but you may notice a small temporary water weight bump. Luteal (Week 4): Progesterone dominates. Cravings intensify (your metabolism actually runs slightly faster), energy dips, and water retention peaks. Understanding these phases means you stop fighting your biology and start working with it.
Nothing is wrong with you — this is physiology. During the luteal phase, your resting metabolic rate increases by approximately 100–300 calories per day. Your body is literally burning more energy, so it signals more hunger. On top of that, progesterone can reduce insulin sensitivity slightly, causing blood sugar to dip faster and cravings for carbohydrates and sweets to spike. Serotonin also tends to drop in this phase, which is part of why comfort foods feel so appealing — carbs temporarily boost serotonin. Rather than white-knuckling through this phase, HerRhythm helps you understand it's happening and plan for it with satisfying, hormone-supportive nutrition choices. Eating a bit more in week 4 is not failure — it may actually be what your body needs.

The follicular phase (roughly days 7–13, after your period ends) is typically when weight loss feels most natural. Estrogen is rising, energy is high, appetite is naturally lower, insulin sensitivity is better, and your mood is more stable. Your body is more receptive to exercise and less prone to cravings. Many women find that a modest calorie deficit feels almost effortless during this window — compared to the luteal phase where the same deficit feels like a battle. That said, "best week to lose weight" is a bit of a reframe HerRhythm wants to shift. The goal isn't to crash diet in week 2 and suffer in week 4 — it's to understand your whole cycle and eat and move in a way that's sustainable across all four phases. Real, lasting weight loss happens over months, not weeks.
Hypothyroidism slows your metabolism, can disrupt your cycle, and makes weight management significantly harder — and most apps completely ignore this. HerRhythm's hypothyroidism overlay adjusts your expectations and insights to reflect the reality of a slower metabolism. It also highlights how thyroid function interacts with your cycle: hypothyroidism can lengthen cycles, worsen luteal phase symptoms, and make water retention more pronounced. The app tracks your energy, mood, and body data without applying unrealistic "average woman" benchmarks. It also gently prompts you to consider things like your medication timing, iodine intake, and whether your fatigue patterns align with thyroid fluctuations versus purely hormonal ones. We always recommend working with your doctor on medication — HerRhythm supports that care, not replace it.

Perimenopause is one of the most underserved phases in women's health apps, and HerRhythm takes it seriously. During perimenopause, estrogen fluctuates wildly, cycles become unpredictable, and the usual hormonal patterns become harder to track. HerRhythm's perimenopause overlay shifts focus from predicting a cycle to tracking your lived experience — symptoms like hot flashes, sleep disruption, mood changes, and unexplained weight gain around the midsection. It contextualizes these symptoms within what's known about perimenopausal hormones rather than treating them as anomalies. Weight management in perimenopause is different: insulin resistance often increases, cortisol sensitivity rises, and muscle mass tends to decline. The app accounts for these shifts so your goals and feedback remain realistic and useful rather than demoralizing.
